Implementing a Support Vector Machine (SVM) In Python








Support Vector Machines are frankly one of the most toughest concepts I have ever attempted to understand and implement. So understanding SVM's can be an overwhelming experience and I suggest that you do not get discouraged while learning them.

So while learning about SVM's on the internet, I watched many lectures. One important lecture was of Patrick Winston of MIT where he developed the idea of SVM from scratch. It involves lot of mathematics and certainly helps ones intution. You can watch that video here but it's certainly not a prerequisite. You can also watch this excellent video by Brandon Rohrer on SVM's and this another one which shows implementation using scikit-learn library.



If you wish you can download the PDF of my handwritten notes about the mathematical derivations of SVM's which I learned about from Patrick's lecture.
